## Lessons learned

During this research I got a few important insights:

1. Problems and niches are supposed to be FOUND, looking EXTERNALLY at the recent trends, market shifts, regulations, employment changes, etc. Not looking INTERNALLY at "What I am good at" or "What market would I want to server". I have to basically forget COMPLETELY about my preferences and strengths and go "SHOPPING" for problems that are WORTHY of solving.
2. It also seems like looking for a **problem** IMPLIES **WHOSE PROBLEM(niche)**. So the process of finding a problem and finding a niche are INTERTWINED and should be done SIMULTANEOUSLY. But I am not 100% sure about it yet.
3. I have a personal bias/weakness which I would call "Academic Break" that makes me want to find a 100% certainty about the problem/niche before proceeding to the next step. While in reality, I should be OK with SUFFICIENT ENOUGH certainty NOT ABOUT THE PROBLEM/NICHE, but about the fact that this problem/niche is WORTHY of FURTHER INVESTIGATION. Meaning that I should be able to PROCEED to the next step with a PROVISIONAL problem/niche HYPOTHESIS and then VALIDATE/INVALIDATE it in the next steps.
4. I realized that I can't imagine my success. I can't visualize myself succeeding in this business. I think it's fine, because I've never have this experience before, but I think it could actually help significantly if I could "see" a successful path forward. I don't know how to fix it yet.
