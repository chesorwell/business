# Research Directive: Deconstructing SMB Freight Brokerage

## Phase 1: The Physics of the Business (First Principles)

_Goal: Understand the fundamental unit of value and the constraints of the system._

**1. The Economic Engine (The "Spread")**

- **Principle:** A broker is an arbitrageur. They sell a "Ride" to a Shipper for price $X$ and buy a "Truck" from a Carrier for price $Y$. Profit = $X - Y$.
- **The Research Questions:**
  - What is the average gross margin % for an SMB broker in 2025? (Target: 12-18%?)
  - What are the unit economics of a _single load_? (e.g., Revenue: $2000, Cost: $1700, Gross Profit: $300).
  - What is the **Cost of Failure**? If a load is stolen/double-brokered, who pays? Does insurance cover it, or is it a deductible that kills the month's profit?

**2. The Assetless Constraint**

- **Principle:** Brokers own _information_, not _trucks_. Their leverage is **Trust** and **Speed**. If they lose trust (hire a thief) or speed (slow to quote), they have zero value.
- **The Research Questions:**
  - What is the "Liability Shield"? Why do Shippers use Brokers instead of going direct? (Hint: Itâ€™s often to outsource the risk of vetting).
  - What happens legally to a broker if they negligently hire a fraudulent carrier? (See: "Negligent Selection" lawsuits).

## Phase 2: The Operational Workflow (The "Happy Path" vs. The "Terror Path")

_Goal: Map the exact sequence of events so we can find the injection point for automation._

**1. The "Happy Path" (Lifecycle of a Load)**

- **Task:** Create a step-by-step flowchart of a standard load.
- **Steps to Investigate:**
  - **Tender:** How does the shipper send the order? (Email? Portal? EDI?)
  - **Coverage:** How does the broker find the truck? (DAT Load Board? Truckstop? Blast email?)
  - **Vetting (THE CRITICAL STEP):** _Exact specific steps._ Do they check FMCSA? Carrier411? Highway? Call the driver?
  - **Dispatch:** Sending the Rate Confirmation (RateCon).
  - **Tracking:** How do they know where the truck is? (Macropoint? Check calls?)
  - **Billing:** Proof of Delivery (POD) -> Invoice.

**2. The "Terror Path" (The Fraud Vector)**

- **Task:** Map exactly how the "Double Broker" scam works in 2025.
- **The Research Questions:**
  - How does a bad guy fake an identity? (Buying "Aged MCs", Spoofing VoIP numbers).
  - What are the "Red Flags" a human broker looks for? (e.g., "Dispatcher has a thick accent but a generic American name," "Gmail address," "Willing to take a low rate").
  - _Why_ does the current software fail to catch this? (Why isn't Carrier411 enough?)

## Phase 3: The Tech Stack (The Environment)

_Goal: Understand where your code will live and what APIs it needs to talk to._

**1. The System of Record (TMS)**

- **Principle:** The TMS (Transportation Management System) is their ERP. It is the source of truth.
- **The Research Questions:**

  - Do they have open APIs?

**2. The Marketplaces (Load Boards)**

- **Principle:** This is where they meet the carriers.
- **The Research Questions:**
  - DAT vs. Truckstop. (Who is dominant for SMBs?)

**3. The Compliance Tools (Your Competitors/Partners)**

- **Principle:** These are the databases they check.
- **The Research Questions:**
  - What does **Carrier411** provide? (Static data).
  - What does **Highway** provide? (Identity data).
  - _Gap Analysis:_ What data is missing that requires a human phone call?

## Phase 4: The "Lingo" (The Camouflage)

_Goal: To sound like an insider, not a developer._

**Task:** Create a glossary of terms. You must know these cold:

- **MC Number / DOT Number:** The social security number of a trucking company.
- **Rate Con:** The contract.
- **BOL (Bill of Lading):** The receipt of goods.
- **Double Brokering:** The specific crime.
- **Factoring Company:** The bank that pays the trucker.
- **Blind Shipment:** When the shipper doesn't know the receiver.
- **Lumper:** Fee for unloading.
