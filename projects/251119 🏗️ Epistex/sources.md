# BOOKS - Agency & Professional Services Foundation

Built to Sell by John Warrillow @ 97 @ 17 @ The essential guide on creating a business that can thrive without you. Teaches productizing services, building recurring revenue, creating systems that eliminate owner dependency, and the Teachable-Valuable-Repeatable framework. Directly addresses the ultimate goal of building an agency that runs and grows without the founder. Story-based approach makes principles easy to implement. Published 2011.

The E-Myth Revisited by Michael E. Gerber @ 98 @ 17 @ The definitive foundational work on why most small businesses fail and how to escape the "technician trap." Teaches the critical distinction between working IN vs ON your business, building systems over people-dependency, creating a franchise prototype that runs independently. Essential for any technical founder transitioning to business owner. Published 1995 but timeless principles.

The Win Without Pitching Manifesto by Blair Enns @ 95 @ 15 @ THE positioning bible for creative/dev agencies. 12 proclamations on how to win clients without free pitching, position as expert (not vendor), lead sales conversations, charge premium rates, and avoid appearing needy. The most cited agency-specific book across all responses. Published 2010 but principles are timeless.

Managing the Professional Service Firm by David H. Maister @ 92 @ 11 @ The 1993 classic that remains the definitive authority on running consultancies and professional services firms. Covers economics, leverage models, marketing, business development, people management, financial metrics, project teams, and multinational strategies. Timeless principles that apply directly to software agencies.

The Business of Expertise by David C. Baker @ 91 @ 11 @ Comprehensive guide on positioning for specialist firms. Teaches vertical vs horizontal positioning, converting insight to impact, pattern matching, expertise-driven growth, and pre/post-tests for positioning decisions. Written specifically for creative/technical agencies by someone who has advised 900+ firms.

Traction (Get a Grip on Your Business) by Gino Wickman @ 93 @ 15 @ The Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS) - the most widely implemented operating system for agencies. Teaches six key components: Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, and Traction. Includes accountability charts, quarterly rocks, level-10 meetings, and scorecards. Used by thousands of 7-figure agencies to systematize operations.

Million Dollar Consulting by Alan Weiss @ 86 @ 11 @ Comprehensive guide to building solo/boutique consulting practices. Covers premium positioning, relationship-based marketing, creating intellectual property, building a brand, and value-based engagement models. 60+ books of accumulated wisdom on consulting businesses. Multiple editions available.

The Pumpkin Plan by Mike Michalowicz @ 82 @ 9 @ Strategy for selective growth - identifying your best clients, firing bad-fit clients, and growing exponentially through specialization. Teaches how to stop spreading yourself thin and focus ruthlessly on your ideal client profile.

# BOOKS - Pricing & Value

$100M Offers by Alex Hormozi @ 94 @ 12 @ Modern bible for creating irresistible offers. Teaches the value equation, pricing psychology, value stacking, guarantee structures, and how to make offers so compelling that clients feel stupid saying no. Directly responsible for thousands of agencies switching to high-ticket pricing models. Available as free PDF or physical book. Published 2021.

Value-Based Fees by Alan Weiss @ 89 @ 10 @ Definitive guide on how to charge based on value delivered rather than time spent. Teaches proposal frameworks, ROI quantification, option-based pricing, and escaping the hourly billing trap. Sample proposals and frameworks included. Essential for charging 10x more as a specialist.

$100M Leads by Alex Hormozi @ 88 @ 9 @ Follow-up on predictable lead generation at scale without being needy. Teaches warm outreach, cold outreach, content marketing, and paid advertising frameworks. Shows how to build lead generation systems that work without appearing desperate. Published 2023.

Pricing Creativity by Blair Enns @ 78 @ 5 @ Comprehensive pricing guide specifically for creative and development firms. Covers value-based pricing implementation, fee structures, pricing conversations, and positioning price as an investment.

Hourly Billing Is Nuts by Jonathan Stark @ 74 @ 4 @ Short manifesto on why developers should abandon hourly billing. Teaches roadmapping, value pricing fundamentals, and positioning expertise. Available as free book/resources. Practical guide for breaking the time-for-money paradigm.

Implementing Value Pricing by Ronald Baker @ 79 @ 2 @ Deep academic and practical foundation for switching from hourly/daily rates to value-based pricing models for professional services. Every profitable custom dev agency owner credits this pricing shift as their biggest revenue leap.

# BOOKS - Positioning & Strategy

Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind by Al Ries & Jack Trout @ 88 @ 10 @ The original 1981 foundational text on positioning theory. Blue Ocean Strategy by W. Chan Kim & Renee Mauborgne @ 67 @ 4 @ Framework for creating uncontested market space. Teaches value innovation, strategy canvas, and how to make competition irrelevant through differentiation rather than competing.

Purple Cow by Seth Godin @ 70 @ 5 @ Manifesto on being remarkable in a crowded market. Teaches why being safe is risky and how to stand out by being genuinely different rather than better.

The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing by Al Ries & Jack Trout @ 70 @ 5 @ Distillation of core marketing principles. Short, punchy lessons on leadership, category creation, focus, and positioning that apply directly to agency marketing.

Focus by Al Ries @ 60 @ 1 @ Book specifically on the power of narrow specialization and owning a category through focus rather than expansion.

This Is Marketing by Seth Godin @ 54 @ 1 @ Philosophy on "people like us do things like this" - building tribes, serving specific audiences, and positioning as the expert for your chosen group.

Crossing the Chasm by Geoffrey Moore @ 61 @ 3 @ Classic on positioning and market strategy, particularly for technology companies navigating early adoption to mainstream markets.

7 Powers by Hamilton Helmer @ 55 @ 1 @ Strategic moats framework. Explains why specialists build defensible positions long-term through scale economies, network effects, counter-positioning, switching costs, branding, cornered resources, and process power.
